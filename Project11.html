UV Curing Chamber: CAD

The Brief

On industrial placement, I was tasked to design a UV curing chamber for 3DP parts. This would be used by all staff so it had to be robust, minimum light leakage and have a door-lock. The electronics and software are discussed in: Project UV Curing Chamber: UV Curing Chamber: Electronics & Software.

Challenges

Making sure there was no light leakage was the main priority for the housing. Inside, there needed to be a way to ensure an even coverage of UV light. I was also restricted to using only laser cut acrylic.

Development

If someone needed to cure a solid block at the maximum print size for a Formlabs 2, they could fit it in the chamber curing area (not sure why someone would do that, but this is designing for extremes). The dimensions of everything else was based from this. I also repurposed a kitchen drawer handle from my bosses’ kitchen as the chamber door handle.

Laser Cutter Experiments

This was not as dangerous as the heading sounds! I tried using the laser cutter at different power/ speed settings to get very deep cuts. The front face had deep grooves in the back where it could slot onto the body. When looking at the chamber front-ways, you could not see any mechanical fixings.

Final Design

The door-lock was a pin slotting into the door controlled by a servo. The electronics were stored underneath the chamber behind the interface panel. One of my more inspired designs, the interface panel is removable: it’s attached with magnets. The turntable was mounted on ball-transfer-units so the stepper motor wasn’t taking any load. The chamber is layered with foil to reflect the UV light.

Further Work

More laser cutter experiments were needed as I still had to file away at the mounting grooves of the front face. On a second iteration, I would like to try designing brackets from laser cut acrylic.

Skills

Just using laser cut acrylic was more of a challenge than I bargained for. However, it forced me to think creatively, like with the interface panel and the front face mounting.
