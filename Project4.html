Card Game Box

The Brief

As one of my favourite card games (which I take with me on holidays and day-trips), I needed a robust and compact box to transport it in. The original box was an enormous cardboard box with a lot of unused space.

Challenges

With most of my carpentry projects, I like to use only recycled wood. It can often be a challenge to make old, used wood look presentable.

CAD Concepts

I made a CAD model to determine the dimensions of the box to find appropriately sized wood scraps. I decided to use a lattice to divide the cards up by category, which also makes game setup much easier. I also wanted to include some felt lining in the box, adding a touch of class.

Final Design

When assembling the final box I decided to use some old jeans to make the straps keeping the panels of the box together. There is recycled bamboo flooring acting as the side panels, the tongue-and-groove being used to locate the panels. The lattice is made from old pine and the panels are made from MDF scraps that I then painted.

Skills

This project tested my ability to match the scrap wood to the design and vice versa. As well as make it look professional yet hand-made.
