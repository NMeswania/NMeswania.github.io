Photo Frame

The Brief

This was originally meant as a gift for displaying a certificate, so I really wanted to use a dark border to create a striking frame around the white paper. I created a few different designs using Photoshop and textures form Pinterest which was super helpful and quick to do!

Chosen Design

I showed the concepts to the gift receiver and they liked this simple double-frame concept.

CAD Models

I only had a rough idea on how to make it, so I headed over to CAD to create the parts where I could work out the size of timber to use. This made the whole sizing process so much quicker than just relying on hand sketches.

The Inner Frame

Here you can the inner frame with its short mitre-lap hybrid joint. The hybrid joint was essential as I was using reclaimed tongue-and-groove panels where I cut away one side of the groove to make a recess along the inner perimeter for the certificate to sit.

The Outer Frame

The outer frame used simple 90° lap joints as they weren’t under any significant load. I was using reclaimed timber that had been previously stained, even after a lot of sanding some darker patches were still visible. Initially, it looked unattractive but, in the end, I think it added a bit more character to the outer frame.

Final Design

And here is the final design. The grey inner frame was covered with a few coats of black and white paint mixture, the wood soaked a lot of the water in the paint allowing the grain texture to come out more. The inner frame sits in the outer frame with just a snug press fit keeping it in place which is plenty strong enough for this application.

Skills

I had a lot of great practice on working away at the wood to create snug and flush joints. Next time, I would try using cleaner timber to avoid a lot of unnecessary sanding!
