Other Photo Frames

Overview

Woodworking is one of my oldest hobbies. I always seek reclaimed timber for all my projects and a perfect use for reclaimed timber is a photo frame. I really enjoy the challenge of designing a frame to match the picture within!

Venetian Painting

A gift for my Dad from Venice. I first started with lap joints then applied the shape using a coping saw and a lot of sanding to get the profile. A router template would have made this a lot faster. The bulges at the corners keep ensure there is enough material for the lap joints.

Inlays on A Small Frame

I had never tried inlays before and this was a great experience. It was a lot of cutting and testing to sneak up to the right fit. I made sure to leave the inlays proud of the frame, so I could sand it flush later. The “P” shape was fiddly, I used my Dremel to gouge out the area for it as my chisels were too big for the curves.

Indonesian Rainforest Painting

A great 1-day build and a very different frame to the previous two. It uses only bamboo rods and string. I soaked the string in a mix of water and wood dye to achieve the mottled dark colouring. The bamboo is held together using just figure-8 knots in the corners. The biggest challenge here was to keep the frame square whilst I assembled it!
