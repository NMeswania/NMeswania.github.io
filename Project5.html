UV Curing Chamber: Electronics & Software

The Brief

On industrial placement, I was tasked to design a UV curing chamber for 3DP parts. This would be used by all staff so it had to be easy and quick to use, with an automatic time-out feature. The mechanical hardware is discussed in: UV Curing Chamber: Mechanical Design.

Challenges

The main challenge was to design the flow of operation to be quick and easy to navigate, whilst also allowing for flexibility so a user could jump in midway through a cure and change the timing. There were a lot of different electronics hardware: a stepper motor + driver, an LCD screen, several control buttons, LED switching + dimming circuitry and a door lock + sensor.

Drawing Inspriation

Taking inspiration from a microwave, I used a turntable that was controlled by a stepper motor. To prevent the stepper motor from over-heating, I decided to turn it 15Â° every 10s, still providing an even coverage of light.

Final Design

First the user would insert the parts, then they would set the cure time then the UV light intensity. The door locks, preventing a user form exposing themselves to UV light. Once the curing had started a user could pause the cure, after which they could cancel or continue. Once the curing finishes, the UV light switch for the white lights and the door unlocks.

Further Work

During the latter half of my placement I gathered feedback on the operation. For example, the ability to continue curing after pausing was a feature added in soon after finishing the design.

Skills

This project helped me better understand user-centred design - understanding what a user of the chamber would want to control and the importance of speed and flexibility of the UI.
