Suburban Cafe Complex

Lo-Fi Jazz-type

I could happily listen to this song on repeat. Mainly because it starts as it finishes with the same chord progression. The not-so-subtle delay-effect change on the piano every few bars giving it that sweeping feel was a complete accident when I was adding in the delay-effect originally.

The section in the middle with all the instruments playing for some reason reminds me of a café in the suburbs in summer, hence the name. Not a clue why. Just vibes you know. I think it’s a mochachino in the picture. Had at in a place in Bristol that did a really great truffle oil infused mac ‘n’ cheese!

Longing for Our Youths

Instrumental Post-Hardcore

Genres are difficult. Well, generally labelling stuff is difficult. I’m not good at genre-ing as you may have noticed. This song is pretty chill and that’s all I wanted in terms of labelling it. It gets the chill vibe from the dulled Wurlitzer-type keyboard and the heap of 7th chords that dominate the song.

I swear there’s logic behind the hypnotically changing colour pattern in the video. It roughly changes based on what an instrument is playing, but it changes instrument depending on the section of the song. As for the name, I got that from the melancholic vibes brought about by how the progression ends on a minor 7th. Probably.

Lost in Place

Instrumental Post-Hardcore

This song is great fun to play! It’s really expressive from the reserved intro to the down-right aggressive choruses. The bass line is also interesting by tying into the guitar lines with arpeggios of sorts (apart from the choruses, kinda needed that rumbling low-end). I reckon a lot of the dynamics and emotion come from the contrasting guitar tones!

I took a lovely walk to Alexandra Park in Bath for this video. About a 2 km walk, it only took me 6 hours (what with stopping to colour and take pictures). I’m really happy with how the song ends with that well-timed awesome view of Bath. The final result was seriously worth the effort!
