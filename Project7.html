Why Is This Project Here?

Despite its non-engineering related topic, this project involved a lot of coding: I learnt Java just to make these music videos. They are made in the Processing environment; a Java-based, open-source Integrated Development Environment aimed at visual arts through coding. Itâ€™s a great bit of software.

What Sort of Coding Did It Involve?

A few songs have FFT analysis which was then styled to match the song. The graphics and pictures were created/ edited in other software packages. Also, each intro-jingle has an audio amplitude analysis which scales the logo to match.

Song: Suburban Cafe Complex

In this video there are 2 types of sound analysis being shown: the two waveforms in the centre of the video are the left and right audio channels, there is an FFT at the bottom of the video. The low bass note is really apparent in the audio waveforms!

Song: Longing for Our Youths (pre-coding)

Before coding started, I created the quarter-window tile patterns in Inkscape. I then created a timing sheet which I could assign how long a pattern would be onscreen for before moving onto the next pattern. I matched the changing of quarter-patterns to the different instruments changing what they play. Each quarter-window of the video roughly represents an instrument. Using a timing sheet (rather than automatically changing) allowed me to be incredibly flexible with the timings.

Song: Longing for Our Youths (coding)

In the code, this timing sheet was imported and cycled through each quarter for the required time calculated in the timing sheet. In tandem with reading the timing sheet and going through the patterns, the FFT was generated and analysed at a completely different rate so the time step of the video had to be very small. It took ~40 minutes to render the video!

Song: Lost in Place

This music video had the least amount of code (and the most amount of walking!). The code here simply counted how many pictures there were then iterated through the pictures at a calculated frequency so the final pictures tied in with the end of the song.

Summary

I hope to make a lot more of these style music videos. Not only is good coding practice, it allows me to exercise creativity in a completely different way - coming up with ideas for a music video that reflects the song and can be created in Processing using coding and a combination of other graphics software. Maybe one day I will make a 3D-effect FFT?
