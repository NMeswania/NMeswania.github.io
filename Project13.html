4-Point Bending Jig

The Brief

One of my earliest CAD projects, I was tasked to design a 4-point bending jig to be used for testing the Young’s modulus of sample materials.

Challenges

Still fairly inexperienced, I had to design this to meet load requirements and be easy to make using milling machines and lathes.

Development

The load requirements were such that the contact point between sample material and jig could deform over time. Therefore, a hardened steel bar would be used, this could not be machined so easily so I designed brackets to hold it in place.

Final Design

The final design is as you see here. It has 4 independently movable supports which are locked down by hefty bolts and slide with the aid of precisely machined T-slots.

Further Work

The next steps are to make and test the design.

Skills

This project greatly improved my CAD skills, it was very useful having an experienced engineer supervising me.
