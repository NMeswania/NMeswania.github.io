Heat Transfer Simulation

The Brief

A University project, I was tasked to analyse the 1-dimensional temperature distribution through a thickness of thermal tile of a space shuttle upon re-entry. I was given outside temperature profiles over time and was asked to calculate the temperature of the inside surface of the tile.

Challenges

The aim of the unit was to use partial differential equations and different numerical methods to solve Fourierâ€™s heat transfer equations. There were also a set of different thermal boundary conditions to test. An additional task was to assess the stability of each methods at different time-steps.

Numerical Methods

The methods to compare were the Forward differencing, DuFort-Frankel, Backward differencing and Crank-Nicolson methods. These would be used in conjunction with a fixed boundary temperature and a zero-heat transfer (zero-gradient) boundary temperature conditions.

Final Design

The final code script took arguments relating to initial tile thickness and required inside temperature. The script would then calculate the tile thickness that met the inside temperature requirement using the shooting-method. The final output being a graph of the heat distribution through the tile over time.

Further Work

The next steps would be to introduce material defects, preferably based on the likely defects that occur in space shuttle tiles.

Skills

Programming was the primary skill used in this project, but I also had to learn about partial differential equations and how to solve them in a discrete time-basis.
